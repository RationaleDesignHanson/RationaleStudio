## Testing Protocol

### 1. Time-to-Comprehension Test
**Method:** Show to 5 strangers for 90 seconds, then ask:
- "What problem does Heirloom solve?"
- "How is it different from competitors?"
- "What was the timeline?"

**Target:** 4/5 correct answers (80% accuracy)
**Current estimate:** 2/5 (40% accuracy)

---

### 2. Executive Scroll Test
**Method:** Scroll at 2 seconds per screen, answer questions:
- "What are the 3 main features?"
- "How long did development take?"
- "What outcomes were achieved?"

**Target:** 70% accuracy
**Current estimate:** 40% accuracy

---

### 3. Visual Density Audit

**Before:**
- Text: 80%
- Visual: 20%

- Text: 40%
- Visual: 60%

## Conclusion

These visual mockups demonstrate the transformation from a **text-heavy developer portfolio** to an **institutional-grade consulting case study**. The key shifts are:

1. **Diagrams over descriptions** (architecture, timeline, journey)
2. **Context over numbers** (baselines, comparisons, sparklines)
3. **Hierarchy over equality** (insight boxes, visual weight)
4. **Outcomes over features** (prove value, show results)
5. **Scanning over reading** (executive-friendly, McKinsey standard)

**Total effort:** 40 hours over 3 weeks
**ROI:** 3x comprehension speed, institutional credibility, executive-ready presentation
